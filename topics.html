Technology in Society

Technology serves as a powerful tool, extending our influence beyond individual lives. It enables engagement in impactful projects, offering the potential to contribute to initiatives that have a global significance. This empowerment through technology aligns with the cinematic notion of individuals making a difference on a grand scale, underscoring the transformative potential of technological advancements.

Sub Topic 1

Social Media Tools

Social media platforms such as Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram not only facilitate connections and idea exchanges but also serve as catalysts for collective action. Notably, movements like #MeToo on Twitter emerged to address and combat sexual harassment, evolving into a global force for social change. However, navigating informed discussions in the age of social media poses challenges, with the prevalence of bot accounts amplifying discord on contentious issues. Instances of automated programs manipulating discussions, such as inflaming the gun control debate in the United States or fueling prejudice-induced violence in Sri Lanka, highlight the need for individuals to acquire the crucial skill of using and critically evaluating social media in order to navigate its complexities.

Examples of Social Media Tools

Buffer

Buffer

Buffer is a social media management platform that allows users to schedule posts, track performance, and manage multiple social media accounts from a centralized dashboard. It supports various social media platforms, including Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Pinterest.

Sprout Social

Sprout Social

Sprout Social is a comprehensive social media management and engagement platform that offers features for scheduling posts, monitoring conversations, and analyzing social media performance. It caters to businesses and agencies looking to manage their social media presence effectively.

Canva

Canva

While not a traditional social media management tool, Canva is a powerful design platform that many use for creating visually appealing social media content. It provides a user-friendly interface with templates for various social media graphics, including posts, stories, and banners.

Sub Topic 2

Crisis-Mapping Tool

The software tool Ushahidi stands as a poignant example of the intersection between technology and society. Originating in response to post-election violence in Kenya, Ushahidi was conceived by two programmers who, prompted by a Nairobi lawyer's struggle to disseminate information about the crisis, created a crisis-mapping tool. This platform aggregates data from various sources like emails, text messages, blog posts, and tweets, transforming them into accessible visual maps. Initially addressing a local crisis, Ushahidi evolved into a free global platform, demonstrating its utility in international disasters. This instance prompts reflection on the broader potential of technology in aiding societies during times of crisis.

Examples of Crisis Mapping Tools

Ushahidi

Ushahidi

Ushahidi is an open-source platform that allows users to crowdsource information and visualize it on a map. It was initially developed to map reports of violence during the 2008 Kenyan election crisis. Users can submit information via various channels such as SMS, email, or web forms, and the data is displayed on an interactive map.

Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team (HOT)

Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team (HOT)

OpenStreetMap is a collaborative mapping project that allows users to create and update maps of any location. In times of crisis, the Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team (HOT) collaborates with volunteers to rapidly map affected areas.

Map Action

Map Action

MapAction is a non-governmental organization (NGO) that specializes in mapping for humanitarian emergencies. While not a tool in the traditional sense, MapAction deploys skilled mapping professionals with the necessary equipment to support humanitarian organizations during crises.
