Technology Connects Us with Others

In this section, we explore the transformative influence of technology on our social fabric. From reshaping our thought processes to revolutionizing how we connect with others and engage in consumption, technology plays a pivotal role in redefining essential aspects of our social dynamics.

Sub Topic 1

Collaborating for the Benefit of Others

The advent of Web 2.0, characterized by interactive web applications that empower individuals to be active contributors, has ushered in a new era of the Internet. Users can easily generate content, fostering connections and collaboration. This shift from passive consumption to active participation reflects a global transformation, supported by the coined term "cognitive surplus," signifying the combination of leisure time and creative tools. Web 2.0's accessibility to media tools and seamless connectivity has facilitated the emergence of projects like Ushahidi, driven by generosity and a shared commitment to information sharing and collaboration.

The Evolution of Web 2.0

Interactive Web Applications

Discover the features that define Web 2.0, including interactive web applications that enable users to actively participate in content creation, sharing, and collaboration. This evolution marks a departure from the static nature of early web platforms.

Cognitive Surplus: Leisure Time and Creative Tools

Explore the concept of "cognitive surplus," a term coined to describe the combination of leisure time and creative tools available to individuals. Web 2.0 harnesses this surplus, transforming it into a powerful force for positive change and collaboration.

Sub Topic 2

Connecting Through Business

Kickstarter provides a platform for individuals to connect and support each other's dreams through direct funding. Users can post their ideas and receive financial backing through crowdfunding, where donors receive rewards based on their pledge levels. This approach involves seeking small donations from a large number of people to generate capital for various projects. Successful Kickstarter ventures range from innovative products like ice chests with integrated blenders to DNA analysis machines for affordable disease diagnosis, along with numerous entertainment projects. With over $3.9 billion raised for businesses, crowdfunding extends beyond entrepreneurial ventures, as platforms like GoFundMe enable people to raise funds for personal needs such as medical bills or tuition.

Examples of Crowdfunding Platforms

Kickstarter

Kickstarter

A platform for creative projects, including films, music, art, technology, and more. Project creators set a funding goal and a deadline, and backers contribute to help reach that goal.

GoFundMe

GoFundMe

A general crowdfunding platform for personal, medical, education, and charitable causes. It allows individuals to create campaigns to raise funds for various needs and is not limited to specific project categories.

Patreon

Patreon

Geared towards creators such as artists, writers, musicians, and podcasters, Patreon allows fans to support their favorite creators on an ongoing basis. Creators offer subscription-based content and rewards to their patrons.

Crowdcube

Crowdcube

A crowdfunding platform focused on equity crowdfunding for startups and small businesses. Investors can contribute money in exchange for equity in the company.
